Who We Are

Organizational Overview

Eco-WASH Liberia (EWL) is a national, non-profit, community-driven organization dedicated to improving public health, environmental sustainability, and climate resilience across Liberia.

We work at the nexus of Water, Sanitation and Hygiene (WASH), environmental conservation, climate change adaptation, and community livelihoods, with a strong emphasis on protecting watersheds, forests, mangroves, rivers, and coastal ecosystems from degradation and pollution.

Through strong partnerships with government institutions, civil society, and local communities, EWL contributes to national development priorities, SDGs, NAPs, and international environmental commitments.

Strategic Objectives

Our roadmap for creating lasting change

Expand equitable access to safe, affordable, and climate-resilient water supply systems in underserved communities.

Improve sanitation and hygiene outcomes using environmentally safe and culturally appropriate solutions that prevent water contamination.

Protect and restore watersheds, forests, mangroves, rivers, and coastal ecosystems critical to water security and livelihoods.

Reduce plastic and solid waste pollution through community-based systems, circular economy approaches, and behavior change.

Strengthen community and local governance systems for water, forest, mangrove, and waste management.

Promote climate-resilient and nature-positive livelihoods, particularly for women, youth, and forest-edge and coastal communities.

Enhance community awareness, advocacy, and policy engagement on WASH, environmental protection, and climate adaptation.

Thematic Program Areas

Integrated solutions for sustainable development

Program Area A

Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH)

  • Borehole drilling, rehabilitation, and solarized water systems
  • Spring protection and gravity-fed water schemes
  • Climate-resilient, gender-responsive, and disability-inclusive sanitation facilities
  • School, health facility, and institutional WASH programs
  • Hygiene promotion, menstrual hygiene management (MHM), and behavior change communication

Ecosystem Protection

Program Area B

Environment, Forests, Mangroves & Blue Economy

  • Community-based forest, mangrove, and coastal ecosystem co-management
  • Mangrove and blue-carbon ecosystem restoration in coastal and riverine areas
  • Watershed, river, and water-source protection
  • Biodiversity conservation and sustainable fisheries support
  • Nature-based climate mitigation and ecosystem-based adaptation
  • Promotion of sustainable blue-economy practices that support livelihoods and water security
Program Area C

Plastic, Solid Waste & Pollution Control

  • Community-based plastic and solid waste collection and segregation
  • River, wetland, forest, and coastal clean-up campaigns
  • Circular economy approaches and recycling initiatives
  • Behavior change campaigns for waste reduction
